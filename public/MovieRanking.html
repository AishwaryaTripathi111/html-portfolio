The Best Movies According To Aish

My Top 3 Movies Of All Times


1. Meenakshi Sundareshwar

Meenakshi Sundareshwar (2021) is a Hindi-language romantic comedy-drama directed by Vivek Soni. The film follows Meenakshi (Sanya Malhotra)
and Sundareshwar (Abhimanyu Dassani), a newlywed couple in an arranged marriage who must navigate a long-distance relationship when
Sundareshwar moves to Bangalore for a job opportunity.

While Meenakshi stays in Madurai with his family, they try to sustain their marriage through phone calls and virtual interactions. The film
explores themes of love, commitment, and the challenges of a long-distance relationship, especially in a traditional Indian setup.

Despite the emotional struggles and misunderstandings, the couple learns to balance personal aspirations and marital responsibilities,
ultimately strengthening their bond. The film is set against the cultural backdrop of Tamil Nadu and blends romance with lighthearted moments.


2. Love at first sight

Love at First Sight (2023) is a romantic drama film directed by Vanessa Caswill, based on the novel The Statistical Probability of Love at
First Sight by Jennifer E. Smith.

The story follows Hadley Sullivan (Haley Lu Richardson) and Oliver Jones (Ben Hardy), who meet by chance at an airport while waiting for a flight
from New York to London. Hadley, a free-spirited American, is dreading attending her father’s wedding in London, while Oliver, a British
statistics enthusiast, is on his way to an important family event. They instantly connect during their flight, sharing deep conversations and
a growing chemistry.

However, upon arrival, they lose track of each other at customs, leaving their potential romance uncertain. With only fate and a few clues to
guide them, Hadley sets out on a heartfelt journey to find Oliver, leading to unexpected twists and emotional realizations.

The film is a sweet and heartwarming take on serendipity, destiny, and how love can blossom in the most unexpected moments.


3. Scream series

The Scream series is a popular slasher horror franchise created by Kevin Williamson and directed (mostly) by Wes Craven. It follows a masked
killer, Ghostface, who taunts and hunts victims, often playing with horror movie tropes.

  1. Scream (1996): Sidney Prescott becomes the target of Ghostface, leading to shocking betrayals and a meta-horror twist.
  2. Scream 2 (1997): Sidney faces a copycat Ghostface while in college, as the killings continue.
  3. Scream 3 (2000): The murders shift to Hollywood, revealing dark secrets about Sidney’s past.
  4. Scream 4 (2011): A new Ghostface emerges a decade later, targeting Sidney and a new generation.
  5. Scream (2022) (Scream 5): Ghostface returns to Woodsboro, connecting new victims to past survivors.
  6. Scream VI (2023): The setting moves to New York, with returning characters facing a deadlier, more brutal Ghostface.

The franchise is known for its mix of horror, satire, and self-aware commentary on slasher films.
